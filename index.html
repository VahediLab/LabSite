Welcome

I am an Assistant Professor at the Department of Genetics and a member of the Institute for Immunology at the Perelman School of Medicine, University of Pennsylvania. In May 2015, I started my research group after five years of research training with Dr. John O'Shea at the NIH. My lab endeavors to understand the biological circuits that underlie cellular processes in immune cells and uncover the molecular basis of major inherited diseases mediated by immune cells. The advent of high throughput genomic and proteomic technologies provides immunologists with a tremendous amount of data. In the face of such a data avalanche, we will develop computational methods to integrate this diverse high throughput data and unravel the basic principles of immunity from a systems level view.
